Aurora-Boutique
Power BI Sales Analysis

Prepared by: Gabriel Okeke | Data Analyst

An interactive retail analytics dashboard developed using Power BI to evaluate sales performance, customer behavior, and product profitability for Aurora Threads Boutique. By leveraging Power Query and DAX, raw transactional data was transformed into meaningful insights, revealing that accessories—especially handbags—drive the majority of revenue, with identifiable peak sales periods. This project provides data-driven recommendations to optimize inventory, refine marketing strategies, and improve customer retention, demonstrating my ability to translate complex data into actionable business insights.

Tools Used

  • Power BI
  • DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
  • Power Query

Dashboard Overview

Key Insights

  • Accessories generate the highest revenue (148K), significantly outperforming Clothing (71K) and Footwear (70K), showing strong category dependency.
  • Handbags and Necklaces are the top-performing products, while Watches and Jumpsuits underperform.
  • Sales peak around Day 5 and Day 25, indicating clear purchasing cycles and high-demand periods.
  • Online and Cash payments dominate transactions, while Debit Card usage remains the lowest.
  • Revenue is concentrated among a small number of high-value customers, with top customers generating up to ~19K.
  • Unisex products contribute the highest revenue (~41.9%), followed by female (~35%) and male (~22%).

Recommendations

  • Focus inventory and marketing efforts on Accessories and top-performing products like Handbags and Necklaces.
  • Launch targeted promotions around peak sales periods (Day 5 and Day 25) to maximize revenue.
  • Develop loyalty programs to retain high-value customers and increase repeat purchases.
  • Improve performance of low-selling products through discounts, bundling, or repositioning.
  • Enhance digital payment systems and incentivize online payments to match customer preferences.
